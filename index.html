Renewable Energy

Renewable energy, often referred to as clean energy, comes from natural sources or processes that are constantly replenished

Since the dawn of humanity people have used renewable sources of energy to survive A little more than 150 years ago people created the technology to extract energy from the ancient fossilized remains of plants and animals. These super-rich but limited sources of energy quickly replaced wood, wind, solar, and water as the main sources of fuel.

  • Nonrenewable energy includes fossil fuels such as oil, gas, and coal.These are available in limited amounts and take a long time to replenish
  • Solar, wind, hydroelectric, biomass, tidal and geothermal power can provide energy without the planet-warming effects of fossil fuels.

Renewable power is booming, as innovation brings down costs and starts to deliver on the promise of a clean energy future

60%

Energy is the main contributor to climate change, it produces around 60 percent of greenhouse gases.

20%

As of 2020, more than 20 percent of power was generated through renewable sources.

3 Billion

More than 40 percent of the world’s population—3 billion—rely on polluting and unhealthy fuels for cooking.

10.3 million

The renewable energy sector employed a record 10.3 million people in 2017.
